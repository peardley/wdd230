Design Principles

Patty Eardley

Repetition

Gatorade Company

gatorade.com

Gatorade's mobile site is a good example of repetition because the logo, symbol, or Gatorade name is repeated throughtout the site so there is no mistaking who's website you are on. The Gatorade lightning bolt serves as the home button for the site. The image with two of Gatorade brand logo with the lightning bolt on it connects it to the icon in the corner. The Gatorade name in the text reiterates the site company.

Alignment

Breyers Company

breyers.com

The mobile website uses center alignment throughout the site to create consistency. The ice cream carton is centered in the middle of the photo along with all the headings, text and button. This adds consistency on the site. The margins are also aligned so the logo and menu edges at the top are aligned with the text and button at the bottom.

Hierarchy

LDS Living Magazine

ldsliving.com

This mobile website uses hierarchy to draw your eyes around the page. Because of the size of the photo your eyes begin there. The bolded text then draws your eye down to the title and then further down to the summary of the article. The LDS Living banner then pulls your eyes up to the top where then you finally rest on the menu options for the site.
