Design Principles

Patty Eardley

Repetition

Gatorade Company

gatorade.com

Gatorade's mobile site is a good example of repetition because the logo, symbol, or Gatorade name is used repetitively so there is no mistaking who's website you are on. The Gatorade lightning bolt serves as the home button for the site. The image with two of Gatorade brand logo with the lightning bolt on it connects the icon in the corner together. The Gatorade name in the text reiterates the company.

Alignment

Breyers Company

breyers.com

The mobile website uses center alignment throughout the site to create consistency. If you notice, the ice cream carton is centered in the middle of the photo along with all the headings, text and button. The margins are also aligned so the logo and menu at the top are aligned with the text and button at the bottom.

Hierarchy

LDS Living Magazine

ldsliving.com

This mobile website uses hierarchy to draw your eyes around the page. Because of the large size of the photo your eyes begin there. The bolded text then draw your eyes down to the title and then further down to the summary of the article. The LDS Living banner then pulls your eyes up where then you finally rest on the menu options for site.
